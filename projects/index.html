Mangrove are salt-tolerant trees and shrubs growing at the margins of sheltered tropical and sub-tropical waters. This location is highly vulnerable to oil spills. Mangroves are also considered to be extremely sensitive to contamination by oil, dependent to a large extent on the substrate in which mangrove are growing. Mangroves grow in dense muddy anaerobic sediments and rely on oxygen supplied through small pores on aerial roots. The mangrove ecosystem can be impacted both by direct effects of the oil and also longer term loss of habitat.
